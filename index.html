TYPES and USES of Interjections


Grammar overview:

Today we mainly focussed on the following:

  1. How interjections help us to express ourselves succinctly in writing.
  2. Interjections are a grammatically independent part of speech. Thus, they can often be removed from a sentence without impacting its meaning.
  3. Punctuation decides the effect of the interjection on the rest of the sentence.
  4. We discussed the following seven types of interjections while reading a conversation between two friends who are reworking on their project on short notice:
    Primary, Secondary, Strong, Mild, Emotive, Volitive and Cognitive

Word meaning:
Ornithologists: People who study the branch of studies dedicated to birds called ornithology.
Use: The phenomena of global warming has led young minds to study its effects on birds and become ornithologists.
Neurons: Nerve cells
Use: There are three types of neurons in our body constantly conducting complex actions to make our brain function.
Nuanced: Characterised by subtle shades of meaning, layers or expression.
Use: Jane Austen draws nuanced characters in her novels.
